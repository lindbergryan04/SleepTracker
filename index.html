I have created a classification model that allows a user to input how many minutes of each activity they engaged in throughout the day, and returns their predicted sleep score given their most similar user. The code on the back end that does the classification is complete but I am still in the process of creating the sliders to allow the user to input their minutes per activity. I made a small multiples visualization that allows the user to select a detailed view version of the heatmap. It has 2 different hover Interactions that show the activity levels users in our dataset through step counts in hours or minutes, with a summary statistic corresponding to each view. I think the most challenging part will be to make sure that there is actually flow and story within our project, since we are working on visuals that are intuitive to use but may not be to the viewer. The most challenging part of our project to design is going to be the interactive portion, where we involve the user by predicting their sleep. We are going to have the user adjust their estimated levels of stress, activity, and hormones so this is definitely going to be challenging to execute.

What Influences a Good Night's Sleep?

Sleep is a cornerstone of our well-being, deeply intertwined with our physical and mental health. But what truly defines a 'good night's sleep'? It's more than just the hours clocked in bed. It involves a complex interplay of factors, from our daily stress and physical activity to the subtle dance of hormones like melatonin (which promotes sleep) and cortisol (which keeps us alert). To gauge sleep quality, we look at several key indicators: Total Sleep Time (TST) ‚Äì the actual amount of time spent asleep; Sleep Latency ‚Äì how long it takes to fall asleep; Wake After Sleep Onset (WASO) ‚Äì the amount of time spent awake after initially falling asleep and before finally waking up; and measures of sleep disruption like Sleep Fragmentation or the Number of Awakenings.

One of the most comprehensive metrics is Sleep Efficiency ‚Äì the percentage of time spent actually asleep while in bed. Let's explore how sleep efficiency varies across different individuals, offering a glimpse into the diverse landscape of human sleep.

For instance, User 12 stands out with an impressive sleep efficiency of 94.23%, achieving 343 minutes of total sleep time with only 21 minutes of Wake After Sleep Onset (WASO). In contrast, User 2 shows a lower efficiency of 73.49%, with 244 minutes of total sleep time and a higher WASO of 84 minutes. These examples highlight the significant individual differences in sleep patterns and quality.

üèÉ Activity

More movement, better sleep. The actigraph recorded physical activity throughout the day to help us understand how users' activity levels might influence their nightly rest. Physical activity is one of the most consistent predictors of better sleep efficiency and duration across individuals.

A 2023 systematic review titled The Effect of Physical Activity on Sleep Quality and Sleep Disorder supports this connection. The review found that regular moderate-intensity physical activity‚Äîsuch as walking, yoga, or cycling‚Äîsignificantly improves sleep quality across diverse populations. The study also emphasizes that while moderate activity during the day can promote restful sleep, vigorous exercise performed too close to bedtime may actually interfere with it.

These findings underscore the importance of monitoring daily activity as part of sleep tracking. Our visualization allows users to explore how their movement patterns might be influencing their sleep outcomes and supports informed behavioral changes to enhance rest and recovery.

Citation: Alnawwar, M. A., Alraddadi, M. I., Algethmi, R. A., Salem, G. A., Salem, M. A., & Alharbi, A. A. (2023). The Effect of Physical Activity on Sleep Quality and Sleep Disorder: A Systematic Review. Available on ResearchGate.
